# Design Calculations

## 1. Wien Bridge Oscillator (Sine Wave Generator)

Target frequency: **50 Hz**

### Formula
f = 1 / (2πRC)

### Component Selection
- C = 100 nF  
- R = 1 / (2π × 50 × 100 × 10⁻⁹)  
- R ≈ 31.83 kΩ  

**Selected standard value:**  
- R = 33 kΩ  

---

### Gain Condition

For sustained oscillations:

Av ≥ 3  
Av = 1 + (Rf / Ri)

Setting:
- Av = 3 → Rf = 2Ri  

**Implemented using:**
- R5 = 24 kΩ  
- R4 = 10 kΩ  

---

### Amplitude Stabilization

- Diodes used in feedback path  
- Prevent oscillation decay and clipping  
- Maintain stable sine amplitude  

---

## 2. Triangular Wave Generator (Carrier)

Target frequency: **~10 kHz**

### Formula
f = R2 / (4 × R3 × R1 × C1)

### Selected Components
- R1 = 2.2 kΩ  
- R2 = 10 kΩ  
- R3 = 10 kΩ  
- C1 = 10 nF  

### Calculated Frequency
f ≈ 11.36 kHz  

---

## 3. Comparator (SPWM Generation)

### Condition
- If Vsine > Vtri → Vout = HIGH  
- If Vsine < Vtri → Vout = LOW  

### Output Characteristics
- PWM frequency ≈ carrier frequency  
- Duty cycle follows sinusoidal reference  

---

## Practical Note

Observed carrier frequency (~5–10 kHz) differs slightly from calculated value due to:

- Component tolerances  
- Op-amp non-idealities  
- PCB parasitic effects  

This behavior is expected in analog implementations.
